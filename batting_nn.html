Intro

MLB batting stats 2010-2016. Data from Baseball-reference.com.

Players with less than 100 plate appearances were removed from the dataset. Pitchers and DHs were also removed.

Using the toy neural network from CS231n at Stanford. I tracked training accuracy for the most likely overall prediction and the top three most likely predictions.

Code can be found here for 8 class classification.

Results

Training Loss (Blue) and Accuracy (Orange: Top 3 / Green: Overall) Plot

34% overall accuracy and 41% top 3 accuracy on test dataset.

Grouping

Given the ~1/3 accuracy, I grouped positions into three arbitrary groups: infield, outfield, and catcher. Obviously, top 3 accuracy is no longer relevant.

Training Loss (Blue) and Accuracy (Orange) Plot

64.9% overall accuracy on test dataset. This is significant improvement over the initial 8-class classification, but suggests that the groups are not optimal or that grouping is not relevant to begin with. Further work should attempt clustering the data to explore.

Given the many heuristics that fans would identify for mapping baseball positions to and from batting stats, i.e. "catchers cannot hit well", one expects significant predictability in this classification task. Our interns at Block Six Analytics were only able to achieve 15% accuracy on this task. The neural network was able to significant outperform them at 34%.
